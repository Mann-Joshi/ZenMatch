## 2023-10-27 - Unified Spatial Traversal for Tile Collision
**Learning:** Mahjong tile collision and freeness checks involve complex spatial math across 3 dimensions. Evaluating top, left, and right blockers as three independent operations resulted in three separate multi-directional array allocations (`getNearbyTiles`) and repetitive iterations over the grid map buckets. This was a significant performance penalty for operations evaluating every tile in nested loops (e.g., hinting, freeness updates).
**Action:** Consolidate Mahjong spatial overlapping checks into a single unified grid traversal loop (`checkIsTileFree`). This tests all directions at once per candidate tile directly from the bucket map, drastically reducing array allocation overhead and halving execution time in performance benchmarks. Always combine localized spatial iterations rather than abstracting them purely for directional purity when looping through massive candidate arrays.
